Top tasks

Top tasks allow a user quick, direct access to information or services that are otherwise buried deeper in content.

Example of top tasks

  • Get a COVID-19 vaccine
  • Changes to testing

When to use top tasks

Top tasks should be used on main topic hub pages. We suggest having no more than 3 top tasks on one main topic hub, but the fewer the better.

They are to be used for shortcuts to content within the topic area that is popular with users or meets a business need.

How to use top tasks

The top tasks should be placed below the H1 and any lede text on a main topic hub page, but above the cards with arrows. This means users will be quickly routed to the popular, or key pages, before seeing other sub topic areas within the hub.

The top task is essentially the small promo card component.

The link in the top task should mirror the heading (H1) of the page it is taking the user to.

The top tasks should be organised in the order of popularity or importance.

They should not direct users to a sub-topic hub or a page just one level down from a main topic hub. A card with arrow component may be more appropriate in these scenarios.

Benefits of top tasks

Research

During testing, NHS.UK's Nav Team found users benefitted from the top task component as it preempted their needs by displaying access to key information that was buried deeper within the hub.

Users felt they were useful if they did not have the time to go on longer journeys, and needed help or information quickly.

This means the components are beneficial in getting users where they need to go, more efficiently.
